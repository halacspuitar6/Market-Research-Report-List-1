<p><p>The pure vanilla market comprises four primary types: beans, extract, powder, and paste. Vanilla beans are the whole pods, prized for their rich flavor. Extract is made by steeping beans in alcohol, providing a versatile flavoring agent for baking and cooking. Powder is produced by grinding dried vanilla beans, offering a concentrated flavor without liquid. Vanilla paste combines ground beans and extract, delivering a thick consistency ideal for recipes needing both texture and intense flavor. Each type serves diverse culinary applications.</p></p>
